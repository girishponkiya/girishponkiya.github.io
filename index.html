About
Applied AI meets research rigour.

I am the sole AI/ML specialist at ISS STOXX's Technology Innovation Lab, where I own the full LLM infrastructure stack end-to-end — from deploying and optimising models on internal GPU infrastructure to building NLP pipelines that process ~700K news articles daily across 10+ languages for ESG controversy monitoring.

My doctoral research at IIT Bombay on Noun Compound Interpretation explored how to automatically uncover implicit semantic relations between nouns — through supervised LSTM paraphrasing, unsupervised approaches with BERT/RoBERTa and T5, and frame-semantic labeling using FrameNet. This work was published at ACL, EMNLP, and COLING.

I also design and deliver a company-wide "Introduction to LLM for Software Developers" workshop series (~70 participants per session), contribute to open source NLP tooling (Hugging Face Transformers, spaCy ecosystem), and have open-sourced a vLLM monitoring dashboard built on Telegraf, InfluxDB, and Grafana.

Research details

The thesis addressed Noun Compound Interpretation (NCI): automatically uncovering implicit semantic relations between component nouns (e.g., student protest → "student is the doer of the protest").

Contributions: (1) An LSTM-based supervised model for prepositional paraphrasing; (2) Unsupervised methods using BERT/RoBERTa (fill-in-the-blank MLM) and T5 (free-form generation) — these unsupervised approaches outperformed prior supervised baselines; (3) FrameNet-based semantic relations for noun compound labeling; (4) Zero-shot learning using FrameNet as a lexical resource via ConvE embeddings.

Relevant courses: Foundation of ML, NLP and the Web, Artificial Intelligence, Topics in NLP (Machine Translation).

Research originated during a 2014 TRDDC internship. Internship mentor Girish K. Palshikar became Ph.D. co-supervisor — a direct intellectual lineage from internship to completed PhD.

Dissertation details

Addressed the Q-MER (Query Maximal Empty Rectangle) problem in external memory — finding the largest axis-parallel empty rectangle around a query point among massive point sets that exceed main memory.

Contributions: (1) Proposed algorithm for an external priority search tree with linear space in log-linear time; (2) Showed all standard range queries can be answered efficiently; (3) Algorithm to solve MER with O(log n) extra space in main memory.
