About

I have a Ph.D. in NLP from IIT Bombay and work as a Data Scientist (VP) at Institutional Shareholder Services. My research focuses on using NLP for financial data collection, especially monitoring ESG controversies.

My doctoral thesis, Noun Compound Interpretation, tackled automatically uncovering implicit semantic relationships within noun compounds (e.g., that student protest implies a student is the doer). I explored supervised LSTM-based models (via distant supervision) for prepositional paraphrasing, and unsupervised BERT/T5-based models for both prepositional and free paraphrasing, as well as abstract labeling using Frame Semantics (FrameNet).

I am passionate about data structures, algorithms, machine learning, and formal verification. I aim to connect research with real-world applications in language processing technologies.
